The Journey

My interest in health and technology started in two defining moments. Watching my grandmother, a doctor, care for me when I was seriously ill showed me the impact compassion and medical knowledge can have on someone's life. Years later, seeing my dad battle diabetes during my freshman year made it personal—I realized that many health challenges are preventable, and that technology could play a huge role in changing outcomes.

That's when things clicked. I started taking computer science seriously, diving into Java and software development while exploring biotechnology through HOSA competitions and lab work. Around the same time, I committed to changing my own health, training consistently at the gym five days a week for the past two years. That discipline taught me something important: real progress comes from showing up consistently, even when it's hard—whether that's in the gym, in the lab, or debugging code at midnight.

Now I'm focused on building skills that let me work at the intersection of these fields, creating solutions that can actually help people live healthier lives.

What I'm Up To
